In this upcoming post I’ll show you how to make blobby shapes out of polygons. Click or tap on the shape below to see what I mean.

To transform the polygon into a blob we’ll make of quadratic Bezier curves. Don’t worry though, you don’t really need to know the math behind Bezier curves in order to use them. If you’re interested in that, you can refer to the bezier curve wikipedia article for details.

Oh look a wild Bezier curve! Try clicking or tapping on it to see what happens.

A quadratic Bezier curve is comprised of three parts:

  1. a start point
  2. an end point
  3. a control point

Here’s another Bezier curve where you can drag around these points.

If you put the start point at the top-left corner, the end point at the bottom-right corner and then move the control point from bottom-left to top-right, it kinda looks like you’re strumming a single-string guitar.

The neat thing about Beziers is that you can string them together to make really curvy shapes.
