polygons to blobs

9/26/2022

Update: I’m still working on this post. I’m just in the process of trying different animations. You can check back now and then to see its progress.

In this upcoming post I’ll show you how to make blobby shapes out of polygons. Click or tap on the shape below to transform it.

The points that define the polygon above are 1,3 5,1 9,4 7,8 2,7 1,3.

To transform the polygon into a blob we’ll make use of the quadratic bezier curve. Don’t worry though, its a pretty simple concept that I won’t be explaining here. You can refer to the mdn documentation on quadratic beziers for more details.

Here are some pretty bezier curves for you to look at. If you don’t like one, trying clicking or tapping it.

A quadratic bezier curve is comprised of three parts:

  1. a start point
  2. a end point
  3. a control point

The neat thing about beziers is that if you’re careful, you can string them together to make really curvy shapes.
